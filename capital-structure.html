Does macroeconomic factors affect the capital structure decisions?

Posted on September 27, 2022

The goal of the managers is to maximize the firm value. But can the managers maximize the firm value through capital structure decision? It has become one of the most researched and discussed topic in the finance world. In 1958, Modigliani and Miller[1] published a landmark paper that suggested firms' value is independent of the capital structure assuming that the capital market is perfect, there is no corporate tax and absence of transaction and bankruptcy costs. This novel finding sparked a lot of debate in the finance domain. Following the paper of Modigliani and Miller, numerous studies have been conducted on this topic which have found that capital structure can affect the firm value.

Now the question remains what factors can affect the capital structure decisions? Past studies suggest that capital structure decisions are affected by firm specific factors like firm size (Rajan & Zingales, 1995[2]), profitability(Rajan & Zingales, 1995[2]; Titman & Wessels, 1988[3]), tangible assets, non-debt tax shields and external macroeconomic factors.

This article focuses on the findings of my thesis titled "Impact of macroeconomic factors on the capital structure of manufacturing firms listed on NEPSE". The study was conducted with the objective to investigate the impact of macroeconomic variables on capital structure of manufacturing firms and to investigate whether the conclusions from past study conducted in other economies relevant in case of Nepal or not. The firms under the study were Bottlers Nepal Ltd. (BNL), Bottlers Nepal Terai Ltd. (BNT), Himalayan Distillery Ltd. (HDL), Nepal Lube Oil Ltd. (NLO) and Shivam Cement Ltd. (SHIVM). The sample period was from 2011 to 2022.
